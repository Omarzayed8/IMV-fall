For instance, an augmented reality encounter might enable users to delve into a historical site, unveiling digital reconstructions of its bygone appearance. This not only offers a more immersive and captivating method of learning about history but also transcends the traditional modes of reading or watching a documentary.

Q2:Database as Symbolic Form was written 19 years ago now. What has changed? What hasn't changed? Did Manovich's predictions come true?

Since the writing of "Database as Symbolic Form" 19 years ago, our interaction with and utilization of databases has undergone substantial transformations. The emergence of social media platforms and the surge in individual-generated data have ushered in novel approaches to organizing and scrutinizing information. Furthermore, the advancement of machine learning and artificial intelligence has enabled more sophisticated analyses of extensive datasets. Despite these technological shifts, Manovich's notions about the significance of databases as a cultural form and their intricate connection to narrative remain pertinent in the present day. The prevalence of the database form in new media persists, evident in numerous popular multimedia encyclopedias and commercial CD-ROM titles structured as collections of distinct items. Manovich's foresight regarding the pivotal role of databases in shaping our comprehension of culture and society has proven accurate. The ascendancy of social media platforms and the escalating volume of individual-generated data have engendered fresh methods for comprehending and scrutinizing social trends and behaviours. Additionally, the utilization of big data, particularly in realms like marketing and politics, has become increasingly pivotal in recent years.

I'm channelling Lev Manovich's vibes to create a personal portfolio website that's more than just a digital resume. I want this space to live with my personality and coding journey, a bit like the interactive narratives he delves into in his "Database as a Symbolic Form" piece. I'm aiming for a website where users input a metaphorical code to navigate through my coding adventures. It's not about following a rigid order but letting users pick and choose, much like Manovich's idea of a non-linear narrative. My vision for this project is all about engagement. I want users to feel like they're right there with me, experiencing different aspects of coding. It's not just about displaying my work; it's about creating an interactive journey that mirrors the way I've learned and experimented with coding.
